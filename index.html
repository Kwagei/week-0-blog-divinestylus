HOW TO CHOOSE THE RIGHT COLORS
FOR YOUR BRAND?

"Colors, like features follow the changes of emotions". These were the words of Pablo Picasso,
who is considered as one of the greatest artists of the 20th century. Color is a key driver of emotions
and emotion is a key driver of action. As a business owner, it is a major concern that your customers
take positive actions toward your brand.

In order to drive such actions, they need to feel the right emotions which are largely influenced by your brand's color.
Therefore, the issue of color picking shouldn't be something you do whenever you feel like it, because color has the
potential to either make or break your business. In fact, research shows that up to 85% of consumers think that color
is an important factor for choosing products, while 93% said that they put most importance on the visual factors when
purchasing products. Yes indeed, we do judge the book by its cover.

Instead of choosing your brand's color based on what seems nice to you or your friends, we're going to walk
through the strategic process ambitious brands use when choosing colors that enables them to stand
out within their market.



HAVE CLEAR GOALS AND A PERSONALITY

Without a clear goal, you're definitely going to drift around in confusion. Ambitious brands with great color schemes
never became that way by drifting.

Toyota red is the main brand color of Toyota. Red is a color of energy, which we'll talk more about in this article.
According to Toyota, it "is highly visible and conveys the Toyota spirit of excitement". Bright shades of red are highly
recognizable and their goal is to create a sense of urgency when customers are viewing Toyota's cars.
Is there any wonder why Coca-Cola also uses red?

What are your brand's goals? What emotions do you want your audience to feel? Choosing your brand's color
is easy if you know what you want to communicate. Think of your company as a person. Who are they?
Are they serious or playful? Classic or modern? Affordable or Luxurious?

Having a clear idea about what your brand's goals are and how you want your audience to feel will help
you to choose colors that have a positive effect on your brand.



RESEARCH YOUR COMPETITION

Facebook, Intel, Barclays, Chase, and Citibank. What do all these companies have in common? First of all,
they share similar industries. The first three are technological companies while the latter three are banks.
More importantly, they all share the color blue. The color blue represents trust, it's no surprise that
companies that hold a good amount of money and data(information) require immense trust from their customers.

Researching the colors of business in your industry can help you decide if you want to stand out by choosing
a different color, or match. However, you can still stand out in the same industry while sharing similar colors.


USE COLOR PSYCHOLOGY

Imagine having the magical powers of making people feel a certain way. That's what color psychology
enables you to do. It helps you reflect on the emotions you want your customers to feel.

This is where you choose colors that match your brand's goals and personality, while also
keeping your competition in mind.

GATHER INSPIRATION AND CREATE A MOOD BOARD/font>

Once you have completed the above three steps, it's time to get inspired. Since you know the colors that best
represent your brand, try finding images and color schemes of those colors. Placing multiple colors together
will give you an idea about the mood and vibe your brand will have.

Once you get your mood board done, you can now build your color scheme. Pick three colors from there.
First your base color, your accent color, and your neutral color. Your Base Color
should reflect on your brand's major personality and also appeal to your target audience.
The remaining colors will be chosen based on how well they match with the base color.

Your Accent Color will be less dominant. This should not only match your brand's
goal and personality but should also pair visually with your base color and appease your audience. The final color
is your Neutral Color. This will most likely be your background color. It's not intended
to draw attention. These include hues of gray, beige whites, and black.

There are no strict rules in this process. However, use this article as a guide in helping you reach a highly
fruitful destination. While these guidelines are tried and true, don't hesitate to use your gut feelings
in the process. Once you are aware of the guidelines, then you can effectively color outside the lines. Be Creative.
